A seven-hire New York day in BigLaw

Thursday, May 28, 2026

Today the story in BigLaw is New York. Seven partner hires landed in the city in a single day, with the headline move being Wilson Sonsini pulling an M&A partner from Weil. That's a head-to-head pull in M&A, and the kind of poach that says where the work is flowing right now.

Around the Wilson Sonsini hire, the city kept adding, Greenberg Traurig in M&A, Davis Polk in antitrust, Dechert in restructuring, Foley & Lardner in transactions, Hinshaw in healthcare, and Eversheds Sutherland in white-collar.

Outside New York, Bradley brought on a real estate partner in Atlanta, Husch Blackwell added in Los Angeles, and Hunton picked up a labor and employment partner in Washington.

One structural read worth your time, separate from the moves: The American Lawyer reports lateral associate hiring outpaced entry-level hires in 2025. Firms are buying experience, not training it.

Sources

Drawn from public reporting by Bloomberg Law and The American Lawyer.
